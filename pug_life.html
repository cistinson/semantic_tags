Is the Pug Life the Life for Me?

Use the information and resources below to determine if a pug is the right fit for you.

Things You Should Know Before Getting a Pug

  • Pugs are LOUD! They wheeze, sneeze, and snore constantly! If you are light sleeper, pugs may not be the best breed for you.
  • Pugs are great companions. Whether you're sitting down for dinner, doing some yoga, or taking a nap, expect your pug to join in. Pugs feel the need to maintain line of sight with their owner at all times.
  • It is imposssible to stay mad at a pug. Their adorable, expressive faces will melt your heart. Even if they've destroyed your favorite stuffed animal, you will have a hard time scolding them. They may even cry when they know they're in trouble.
  • At least once per day a pug will become possessed causing them to sprint full speed in circles around the living room. This has been deemed the "pug run" and will last for approximately two to three minutes. Once the exhausted pug has snapped back to reality they will likely take one of their three naps for the day.
